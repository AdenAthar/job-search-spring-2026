# Reflection

## Activity Description

The goal of this assignment was to run a real, structured job search targeting entry-level SWE and ML/AI engineering roles. I set up a tracking spreadsheet and systematically applied to 25+ positions. Roles ranged from general software engineering to specialized ML positions at companies like Microsoft, NVIDIA, Meta, Salesforce, and a range of startups. The expectation I set for myself was to treat each application seriously — actually reading the job description, checking whether my background was a genuine fit, and adjusting materials where it made sense. As the campaign progressed, I also revised my resume in collaboration with my supervisor to better reflect my contributions to the Granny open-source project, which became a stronger talking point for ML-focused roles.

## Technical Decisions

The biggest decision was how to split my time between SWE-focused and ML-focused roles. I ended up keeping both tracks open intentionally, my background spans web dev, systems work, and applied ML research, so narrowing too early felt like leaving options on the table. The tradeoff is a harder narrative to maintain, so I ended up with two resume versions and switched between them depending on the role type.

I also made a deliberate decision to target a mix of company sizes — large companies like Microsoft and Salesforce alongside smaller ones like Thehive.ai and FreshConsulting. The reasoning was that large companies offer structure and credibility early on, while smaller ones tend.
